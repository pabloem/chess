Visualizing Chess

Chess is awesome. I grew up playing it with my brother, my mother, and later on with friends and coworkers. I never became good; but I did enjoy it a lot.

Who captures who?

Who captures you...

Here you can see in what proportion a piece is captured by other pieces. You can click on the pieces on the left, and a chart will be drawn showing you how often the pieces on the left are captured by the pieces on the right.

For instance, a pawn that has been captured, has a 47% chance of having been captured by another pawn - whereas a rook has only a 7% chance of having been captured by a pawn.

Who do you capture...

The following graph is an inverse of the previous one. It represents how often one piece captures a piece of a different kind.

For example, 73% of the pieces that pawns capture are other pawns, and only 2% are queens.

The Path to Crowning

We saw from Oliver Brennan's Chess piece survival rates how probable is that a chess piece will survive or be captured. Here, I observe where pawns are most likely to have been captured. As we see, most are captured in the center of the board. Note that 0% at the end of the board - this is because pawns are crowned there - This visualization does not show whether the resulting crowned piece survives its first turn.

It is interesting that Black pawns tend to be captured earlier on their path to crowning, as opposed to white pawns that generally bite the big one further along the road.

Of course, pawns don't always die the same way on each square. For instance, both white and black pawns die the most on their second squares (square 4 and 5). Of this 37%, 0.4% for white and 1% for black are captures en-passant!
